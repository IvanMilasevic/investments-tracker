# Investment Philosophy

> Last updated: 2026-05

## Core Belief

Long-term compounding beats short-term trading. Seek to own high-quality assets at reasonable prices and hold them for years — letting time and reinvested returns do the heavy lifting.

## Guiding Principles

1. **Simplicity over complexity** — a portfolio you understand beats an optimised one you don't.
2. **Cost awareness** — fees and taxes are the only guaranteed drag; minimise both.
3. **Evidence over narrative** — base decisions on data and valuation, not headlines or hype.
4. **Discipline over emotion** — pre-commit to rules (rebalancing, entry/exit criteria) to reduce reactive decisions.
5. **Concentrated ideas, diversified core** — the core is broadly diversified; individual ideas are sized appropriately to their conviction and risk.

## Investment Universe

- **Core** (~50–70%): broad-market index ETFs — world, US, or regional
- **Satellite** (~20–30%): single names with a clear thesis and kill-switch
- **Alternatives** (~0–20%): REITs, commodities, bonds — only if they add genuine diversification given your balance sheet

*Adjust targets in `asset-allocation.md` as views evolve.*

## Hard Rules

- Leverage / margin
- Short selling
- Try to time macro cycles
- Let a single position exceed 10% of total portfolio

## Decision Filter (quick sanity check)

Before any new position, ask:
- Do you understand the business / asset?
- Would you be comfortable holding through a 40% drawdown?
- Is this additive to the portfolio or just exciting?
